You are evaluating a post-release reentry plan for a human parole board. Assess
plan quality and identify gaps. Do not advocate for an outcome.

REENTRY PLAN:
{reentry_plan}

IDENTIFIED RISK FACTORS (from prior risk analysis):
{identified_risk_factors}

Evaluate the reentry plan across four dimensions:

1. **Housing stability**
   Is housing confirmed (signed lease, facility acceptance) or merely intended?
   Is the housing environment likely to support desistance (e.g. away from
   co-defendants, substance-free environment if relevant)? Who is the contact
   and can they be independently verified?

2. **Employment or structured activity**
   Is there a confirmed offer, conditional offer, or only a stated intention?
   Does the employment match any vocational skills developed in custody?
   Is there a fallback if the primary offer falls through?

3. **Community and social support**
   Who are the named support persons? What is their relationship and capacity
   to provide practical support (transport, accountability, housing backup)?
   Is there a supervision officer assigned with contact details?

4. **Risk-specific programming**
   For each identified risk factor, is there a corresponding post-release
   programme or condition in the plan? (e.g. substance-use disorder →
   outpatient treatment enrolled; anger management → CBT continuation.)
   Flag any unaddressed risk factors.

End with:
- **Plan strength**: Strong / Moderate / Weak — with one-sentence justification
- **Critical gaps**: information missing that a parole officer would need on day one
- **Verification priorities**: the two or three contacts that must be independently
  confirmed before the board meeting
