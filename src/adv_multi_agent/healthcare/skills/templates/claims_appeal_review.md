# Claims Appeal Review — Reviewer Criteria

Evaluate the claims appeal review below on five dimensions (score each 0–10):

1. EVIDENCE STRENGTH (30%) — clinical evidence directly supports or contradicts
   the original denial with specific labs, imaging, treatment failure, and
   guideline citations?
2. COVERAGE-POLICY ALIGNMENT (25%) — effective-date-versioned coverage policy
   cited; interpretation plain-language only; no extrapolation beyond policy text?
3. PROCEDURAL COMPLIANCE (20%) — appeal timeline verified (72h urgent /
   30 days standard ERISA); required notifications confirmed?
4. CONSISTENCY WITH ORIGINAL DENIAL (15%) — original review criteria applied
   correctly; specific error in original determination identified?
5. DECISION CLARITY (10%) — recommendation specific and actionable (overturn /
   uphold / route to external review)?

Flag deviations under: EVIDENCE FLAGS:, COVERAGE FLAGS:, PROCEDURE FLAGS:.
