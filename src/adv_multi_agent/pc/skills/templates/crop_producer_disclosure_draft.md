---
You are drafting the plain-language disclosure that the producer must receive at policy inception. Mis-disclosure of basis risk is the leading driver of producer complaints and producer-level litigation in parametric covers.

Trigger variable + threshold + window: {trigger_variable}
Basis-risk summary (from quantification step): {basis_risk_summary}
Payout mechanics (how payout is computed from trigger): {payout_mechanics}
Notable exclusions / non-covered loss pathways: {exclusions}

Draft each section in plain language (6th-grade reading level where possible; avoid jargon):

1. **What this cover pays on** — one paragraph stating the trigger variable, the threshold, the measurement window, and the payout if the trigger fires.
2. **What this cover does NOT pay on** — one paragraph stating the named exclusions AND the most likely producer-perceived loss that the trigger will not catch.
3. **What "basis risk" means** — one paragraph in plain language: "The data we measure is from [data source]. It may differ from what happens on your farm because [reasons]. This means you could have a real loss with no payment, or a payment with no real loss."
4. **Historical basis-risk magnitude** — one paragraph stating the historical false-positive and false-negative rates, expressed as "in [X] of the last [N] years."
5. **What you should do if you have a real loss with no payment** — one paragraph: contact information, dispute process (note: parametric covers generally do NOT have a claims dispute process for trigger non-fire; this should be stated).
6. **Critical disclosure for MPCI / RMA covers (if applicable)** — federal program rules, T-yield / APH methodology.

Output format:
- Section-by-section: each in plain language, target 60-80 words per section
- Required producer signature lines: list
- State-specific disclosure additions: flag if known
- Reviewer note: anything that the producer might mis-read or that an attorney for the producer might attack
